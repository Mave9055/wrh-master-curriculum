### **FACILITATOR SCRIPT**

#### **[0:00 – 8:00] THE ANCHOR**
**Purpose:** Re-establish safety and control.

*(Walk straight to center. Eyes locked on the room. Energy sharp.)*

"Yellow light. Red light. You run this room. Feet into the floor, blow out through the straw if your system lights up.

Season 1 is complete. You’ve seen the full Loop. Today we begin Season 2 — The Drivers. We go under the hood. We find out why the machine refuses to shut off even when the mind knows better. This session is called **The Body Keeps Score**. The body never forgot the original wiring."

---

#### **[8:00 – 42:00] THE EPISODE — The Body’s Hidden Ledger**
**Purpose:** Explaining somatic memory and the disconnect between mind and body.

*(Lean in. Voice intense, like you’re opening a locked vault.)*

"The Architect’s mind says, ‘I’m safe now.’ His body says, ‘Bullshit.’

The nervous system still carries the exact imprint of the Glass Box — thirty days of alarms, lights, pain, and zero consistent human touch. It still carries the exact imprint of the blanket night at age six — sudden violence, zero warning, freeze or die.

Those experiences didn’t just happen and go away. They were burned into the nervous system like firmware. So years later, when the kitchen is quiet or the apartment is calm, the body doesn’t read “safety.” It reads “threat incoming.”

The Hum isn’t anxiety. It’s the body’s ledger keeping score. Every heartbeat that never got the “all clear” signal is still running the old program. The crash isn’t exhaustion. It’s the body enforcing the only rule it trusts: when danger feels close, shut everything down.

The disappearing act isn’t weakness. It’s the body protecting itself exactly the way it learned to survive the incubator and the blanket. The mind can know the war is over. The body is still in combat. And the body always wins the argument."

*(Beat. Room is locked in.)*

"That’s why the Loop is so hard to break. The mind wants to change. The body is still running on forty-year-old survival code."

---

#### **[42:00 – 67:00] THE MECHANISM — Nervous System Memory**
**Purpose:** The diagnostic mapping of somatic records.

*(Walk to whiteboard fast. Draw the body’s ledger live while you talk — keep the energy moving like you’re exposing classified schematics.)*

"Here’s the exact mechanism. This is how the body keeps score and why it overrides the mind every time."

**(Draw and connect arrows in real time — big, clean, fast):**  
`Original wiring (Glass Box + blanket night) → Nervous system records the threat as permanent data → Body creates its own ledger: “Danger is the baseline” → Mind says “safe” → Body says “not yet” → Hum stays on → mask activates → crash → disappear → Miss → shame → Ledger updates with new proof: “See? I was right” → Loop reinforced at the body level.`

"This is nervous system memory. Not thoughts. Not emotions. Pure physiological data stored in the body. The reason the Architect can’t just ‘think positive’ or ‘try harder’ is because his body is still living in the Glass Box and under the blanket.

The ledger doesn’t care what the calendar says. It only cares what it recorded at the moment the wiring was installed."

*(Leave the board up. Step back. Eyes scanning the room.)*

"The body doesn’t lie. It just keeps the score from the original war."

**Purpose:** Recognition without forced disclosure.

*(Direct. Low, intense voice. Zero pressure.)*

"You don’t have to say a word. Just notice: If your body reacts before your mind can catch up… if calm feels dangerous… if the Hum kicks in even when nothing is happening… that’s not broken. That’s the ledger. That’s nervous system memory showing you exactly why the Loop keeps winning."

#### **[72:00 – 75:00] THE SHIFT + CLIFFHANGER**
**Purpose:** Re-ground. Bridge to next session.

Next session we break down the difference between a real trigger and an activation. Why something tiny can flip the entire system in half a second. And why the body doesn’t need an actual threat — it just needs the memory of one.
