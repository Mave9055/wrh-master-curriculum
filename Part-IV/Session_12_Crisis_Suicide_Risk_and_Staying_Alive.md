# SESSION 12: CRISIS, SUICIDE RISK, AND STAYING ALIVE
## Part IV: The Operational Package

### **Session Overview**
**Goal:** Understand suicidal ideation as a physiological regulation strategy of an exhausted nervous system. Provide tools for safety planning and "time and distance" mitigation.
**Methodology:** System Logic Translation / Interpersonal Theory of Suicide (IPTS)
**Case Study:** The Architect (Daniel)
**Time:** 80 Minutes

---

**VA Program Alignment:** Suicide Prevention (SP); Serious Mental Illness (SMI); Moral Injury Support.
**Evidence-Based Mapping:** Interpersonal Theory of Suicide (IPTS – Joiner); Safety Planning Intervention (SPI – Stanley & Brown); VA/DoD CPG Version 3.0 (April 2024).

### **FACILITATOR SCRIPT**

#### **[0:00 – 10:00] OPENING GROUNDING & RGP**
**Purpose:** Establish safety and physiological baseline.

"Welcome to Session 12. This session is called *Crisis, Suicide Risk, and Staying Alive*. Before we go anywhere, I want to name something directly: **This session is not about making you promise to stay alive.** It’s not about guilt, or shame, or ‘reasons to live.’ It’s about understanding *why* your nervous system might produce the thought of disappearing—and what that thought actually is.

A thought is not a plan. A thought is not a moral failure. A thought is data.

**RGP exercise:** Close your eyes or lower your gaze. Take a breath in for 4 counts… hold for 2… out for 6. Again. In 4, hold 2, out 6. One more time. Now just notice. Without changing anything—just notice what you feel. Is there tension somewhere? A heaviness? A numbness? An urge to move?"

---

#### **[10:00 – 22:00] LECTURE 1 – NERVOUS SYSTEM IN CRISIS**
**Purpose:** Physiology of ideation.

"Let’s talk about what happens in the body when suicidal ideation shows up. Most people think suicidal thoughts are a problem of *meaning*—‘I have no reason to live.’ But they come from a nervous system that has been in survival mode for so long that it starts to run out of options.

When you experience chronic stress, your sympathetic nervous system (fight/flight) stays on. Over time, that system exhausts. So the nervous system tries the other extreme: **dorsal vagal shutdown**. That’s the ‘freeze’ or ‘collapse’ response.

**Suicidal ideation often lives in the space between those two states**—the oscillation from wired to numb to wired again. The brain starts to look for a permanent off switch. Not because you’re weak. Because your nervous system is exhausted."

---

#### **[22:00 – 32:00] LECTURE 2 – NSSI AS REGULATORY STRATEGY**
**Purpose:** Reframe self-harm as a survival tool.

"Some people turn to non-suicidal self-injury (NSSI)—cutting, burning, hitting. **NSSI is a regulation strategy.** A dangerous one, but a logical one. When you injure yourself, your body releases natural painkillers. It creates a ‘shocking calm.’

**Key clinical point:** While the goal of NSSI is the ‘shocking calm,’ we must name the clinical reality: recent self-harm is one of the highest statistical predictors of a future suicide attempt. The nervous system is learning to use pain as a solution, and that is a loop we must interrupt."

---

#### **[32:00 – 40:00] MODULE A – MEANS SAFETY EDUCATION**
**Purpose:** Time and distance mitigation.

"Most suicide attempts happen within 10 minutes of the decision point. That means the single most powerful tool you have is **time and distance**. This is not about willpower. This is about physics. If the means are not immediately available, the nervous system has a chance to regulate.

**Three actions:**
1. **Secure firearms** – Add a 10-minute barrier.
2. **Medication pause** – Keep only a 3-day supply.
3. **Environmental check** – Remove loose sharp objects."

---

#### **[40:00 – 55:00] LECTURE 3 – PERCEIVED BURDENSOMENESS & ACQUIRED CAPABILITY**
**Purpose:** The psychology of the "Ember."

"Dr. Thomas Joiner’s theory says suicide happens when **perceived burdensomeness** and **thwarted belongingness** meet **acquired capability**.

Perceived burdensomeness is the belief that your existence is a liability. It often overlaps with **Moral Injury**. Acquired capability is the ability to overcome the fear of death through repeated exposure to pain or violence. Military service teaches the nervous system that pain is survivable. It lowers the fear barrier.

If you have had suicidal thoughts, it is because your nervous system has learned that pain is tolerable and your absence might relieve others. That is a logical conclusion from bad data."

---

#### **[55:00 – 70:00] SAFETY REFERENCE CARD – GUIDED COMPLETION**
**Purpose:** Tangible safety tool.

"Take out the card. We’re filling it out together.
1. **My personal warning signs** – The small stuff. 'I stop returning texts.'
2. **Coping strategies I can use alone** – Cold water, wall press, walking.
3. **People and places that help** – List two people and one safe place.
4. **Professional resources** – 988 Press 1.

**Final step:** Turn the card over. Write one sentence—your reason to stay for the next 24 hours. Small is fine. 'My dog needs fed.'"

---

#### **[70:00 – 75:00] CASE STUDY – THE ARCHITECT’S EMBER**
**Purpose:** Emotional integration.

"Daniel—The Architect—lived in this wiring. From the NICU to the deaths of 23 people he loved. He was suicidal, convinced he'd failed his kids. But the ember still burned. Fueled by a stubborn refusal to give up. You don’t have to share your story. But if any of that felt familiar, you are not alone."

---

#### **[75:00 – 80:00] CLOSING GROUNDING & CHECK-OUT**
**Purpose:** Safe dismissal.

"Feet on the floor. Hands on thighs. Breath in for 4… hold… out for 6. Say one word that describes how you’re feeling right now. No explanations. Just the word.

Put your Safety Reference Card in your wallet. Take it with you. You're free."
