# SESSION 12: CRISIS, SUICIDE RISK, AND STAYING ALIVE
## Part IV: The Operational Package

### **Session Overview**
**Goal:** Understand suicidal ideation as a physiological regulation strategy of an exhausted nervous system. Provide tools for safety planning and "time and distance" mitigation.
**Methodology:** System Logic Translation / Interpersonal Theory of Suicide (IPTS)
**Case Study:** The Architect (Daniel)
**Time:** 80 Minutes

---

### **ADMINISTRATIVE HEADER & RISK MANAGEMENT**
**VA Program Alignment:** Suicide Prevention (SP); Serious Mental Illness (SMI); Moral Injury Support.
**Evidence-Based Mapping:** Interpersonal Theory of Suicide (IPTS – Joiner); Safety Planning Intervention (SPI – Stanley & Brown); VA/DoD CPG Version 3.0 (April 2024).

**Liability Boundary (Read aloud at start):**
"This session delivers trauma-informed psychoeducation on suicide risk mechanisms and nervous system responses. It does not replace clinical assessment, crisis intervention, diagnosis, or psychiatric care. I recognize dysregulation, deploy grounding, and enact warm handoff."

### **FACILITATOR SCRIPT**

#### **[0:00 – 10:00] THE ANCHOR**
**Purpose:** Establish safety protocol and physiological baseline.

*(Walk to the center of the room. Stand still. Sharp eye contact.)*

"Welcome to Session 12. This session is called *Crisis, Suicide Risk, and Staying Alive*. Before we go anywhere, I want to name something directly: **This session is not about making you promise to stay alive.** It’s not about guilt, or shame, or ‘reasons to live.’ It’s about understanding *why* your nervous system might produce the thought of disappearing—and what that thought actually is.

A thought is not a plan. A thought is not a moral failure. A thought is data.

Feet flat on the floor. Hand on chest, hand on belly. In for four… hold… out for six. Do it. Again. Good. Now just notice. Without changing anything—just notice what you feel. Is there tension somewhere? A heaviness? A numbness? An urge to move?"

---

#### **[10:00 – 22:00] THE EPISODE — The Exhausted System**
**Purpose:** Physiology of ideation and the Architect's story.

*(Lean in. Voice drops to an intense, technical tone.)*

"Let’s talk about what happens in the body when suicidal ideation shows up. Most people think suicidal thoughts are a problem of *meaning*—‘I have no reason to live.’ But they come from a nervous system that has been in survival mode for so long that it starts to run out of options.

The Architect spent years in this wiring. From the NICU to the deaths of 23 people he loved, his sympathetic nervous system was permanently 'on.' But you can't stay in fight/flight forever. Eventually, the system exhausts. It tries the other extreme: **Dorsal Vagal Shutdown**. That’s the ‘freeze’ or ‘collapse’ response.

Suicidal ideation often lives in the space between those two states—the oscillation from wired to numb to wired again. The brain starts to look for a permanent off switch. Not because you’re weak. Because your nervous system is exhausted."

---

#### **[22:00 – 40:00] THE MECHANISM — Regulation vs. Destruction**
**Purpose:** Reframe self-harm and the "Time and Distance" physics.

*(Walk to the whiteboard. Draw the 'Regulation Loop' live while you talk.)*

"Some people turn to non-suicidal self-injury (NSSI)—cutting, burning, hitting. **NSSI is a regulation strategy.** A dangerous one, but a logical one. When you injure yourself, your body releases natural painkillers. It creates a ‘shocking calm.’ 

But here is the exact mechanism of the danger: the nervous system learns to use pain as a solution. We have to interrupt that loop with **Physics**.

Most suicide attempts happen within 10 minutes of the decision point. That means the single most powerful tool you have is **Time and Distance**. If the means are not immediately available, the nervous system has a chance to regulate.

**(Draw three barriers on the board):**
1. **Secure Firearms** (10-minute barrier)
2. **Medication Pause** (3-day supply only)
3. **Environmental Check** (Remove loose sharps)"

---

#### **[40:00 – 55:00] THE MIRROR — Perceived Burdensomeness**
**Purpose:** Internal recognition of Joiner's Theory and Moral Injury.

*(Direct. Low, intense voice. Zero pressure.)*

"Dr. Thomas Joiner’s theory says suicide happens when **Perceived Burdensomeness** meets **Acquired Capability**. 

Perceived burdensomeness is the belief that your existence is a liability. It often overlaps with **Moral Injury**—the wound that occurs when you believe you have violated your own core values. Acquired capability is the ability to overcome the fear of death through repeated exposure to pain or violence. 

If you have had these thoughts, it is not because you are weak. It is because your nervous system has learned that pain is tolerable and your absence might relieve others. That is a logical conclusion from bad data. The machine is just showing you its exhausted code."

---

#### **[55:00 – 75:00] THE SHIFT — The Safety Reference Card**
**Purpose:** Re-ground and provide a tangible tool.

*(Stronger voice. Lean forward.)*

"Here is your tool for right now: The Safety Reference Card. We’re filling it out together.
1. **Warning Signs:** The small stuff. 'I stop returning texts.'
2. **Coping Strategies:** Cold water, wall press, walking.
3. **Resources:** 988 Press 1.

**The Ember:** Turn the card over. Write one sentence—your reason to stay for the next 24 hours. Small is fine. 'My dog needs fed.' Daniel—The Architect—had his ember fueled by a stubborn refusal to give up. You don’t have to share your story. But if any of that felt familiar, you are not alone."

---

#### **[75:00 – 80:00] THE CHECK-OUT**
**Purpose:** Safe dismissal.

"Feet on the floor. Hands on thighs. Breath in for 4… hold… out for 6. Say one word that describes how you’re feeling right now. No explanations. Just the word.

Put your Safety Reference Card in your wallet. Take it with you. You're free. See you next session."
